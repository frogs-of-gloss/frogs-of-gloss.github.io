saltsitting by Imani Elizabeth Jackson

A teaching, a reaching for, a dash to taste. Making place out of a hole in the earth, would be better if you had a lamp to read by. Jackson aims her warm and steady light straight at the surface of things, making plain the long shadows our taking makes. Like a prism held close to the eye, saltsitting unlocks with each turn of its angles: musical, medicinal, material inquiries into the lines that drift between erosion and recuperation.

A shift in temperature, a drop in climate, pink sound

The rain’s music always be more insistent than the drumming

across the way / and she don’t know the stomach from the

core of the earthworks, or the difference between mining and

the mine she’s made / This breathplace / A drum beat can get

off / lose its rhythm

Rain or no rain, it’s salinous cold and time to eat, to breathe

(it is rhythm)
