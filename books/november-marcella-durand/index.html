GLOSS

books light about

November by Marcella Durand

description

11.3.20

Birds rhyme with obsession

and stillness.

I focus on

a stand of grass

that, like the Americas,

looked “empty” or

were presented as

such to facilitate the grabbing

of the blood/gold/bodies.

Ripe stands of goldenrod

go to fuzz and seeds,

stripes of vegetation give

way to shape and movement—

my eye focuses and seeds

and stripes resolve

to an entire flock

of birds that turn

out to be “rare,” but

in the endless big city,

every bird is rare—

the lizard-like

northern flickers on

high oak trees, all red

and spots and brown,

yellow-shafted tails, yellow

of which brings me to

the yellow-rumped warblers,

long and sharp-eyed, the

expression in their eyes

so different from the cute

round-eyed kinglets, feathery

circles in flight with bright

tufts as ruby or gold.

All these birds in solidago,

stalks growing on concrete

through tilting chain-link fences

that rattle with wind and

passing bicyclists—a woman

between the stands of flowers

and the river gathers armfuls

of something—nettles? mugwort?

There is much mugwort

to be had here; it spreads

quickly and grows tall. Along

the highway are hidden gardens

of squash and kale—like the birds,

invisible, in a space they are not,

and twigs actually garden tools.
